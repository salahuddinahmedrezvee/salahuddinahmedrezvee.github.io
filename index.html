Salahuddin Ahmed

Materials Characterization | Printable Electronics | Microfabrication

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Engineering Science and Mechanics at Penn State University, working on materials development, device fabrication, and advanced characterization for biomedical applications. My research focuses on designing conductive polymer and liquid-metal composite systems, fabricating soft and microstructured electronic devices, and validating their mechanical, electrical, and interfacial performance for human-integrated applications. My work spans printable electronics, lithography-based microfabrication, and bioadhesive interface engineering. I develop material systems, translate them into functional devices, and systematically characterize their structure–property relationships using microscopy, spectroscopy, thermal analysis, rheology, and electrical testing. These efforts support the development of scalable, reliable bioelectronic platforms for neural and wearable biomedical systems.

Project

Stick-and-Play Bioadhesive Hairlike Electrodes for Chronic EEG (npj Biomedical Innovations, 2025)

  • Fabricated hairlike EEG devices using DIW 3D printing for reproducible multilayer device construction
  • Built a 4-layer architecture: PDMS bottom/top encapsulation, conductive polymer hydrogel electrode, and printed bioadhesive layer
  • Used a conductive polymer hydrogel electrode system based on PEDOT:PSS and hydrophilic polyurethane (PU)
  • Designed a biomimetic “hairlike” interconnect geometry (thin, narrow traces) to blend with hair and minimize visibility
  • Engineered a UV-polymerized bioadhesive (PU–PAA) and functionalized it using EDC/NHS chemistry to strengthen skin bonding
  • Validated adhesion stability and interface reliability across daily-use conditions and repeat loading/handling scenarios

Printable Liquid-Metal Composite Conductors & Functional Interconnects

  • Formulated printable liquid-metal composite conductors for patterned interconnects and device wiring
  • Optimized print/process parameters to improve line fidelity, continuity, and electrical repeatability
  • Correlated microstructure and dispersion state with electrical behavior using microscopy and spectroscopy

Microfabrication: Lithography-Based Patterned Metal / Mesh Structures

  • Executed cleanroom photolithography workflow using spin coating, exposure/alignment, and pattern development
  • Fabricated micro-patterned metal structures for device-level layouts and mesh-style geometries
  • Performed process iteration to reduce pattern defects and improve feature uniformity

Tools I Used (Characterization & Validation)

  • Microscopy / Surface: SEM, EDS mapping, AFM
  • Spectroscopy: FTIR (Bruker V70), Raman (LabRAM HR), XPS
  • Structure / Thermal: XRD, DSC, TGA
  • Rheology / Printability: shear-thinning and recovery characterization for DIW inks
  • Electrical: resistance/conductivity checks; interface/impedance-style validation for electrode systems
