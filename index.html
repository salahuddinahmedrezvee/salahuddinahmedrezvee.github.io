Projects

Self-Assembly Enabled Printable Asymmetric Self-Insulated Conductor (aSISC)

Developed liquid-metal based conductive composite systems for stretchable electronics. Designed asymmetric self-insulated architecture eliminating secondary encapsulation. Validated electrical stability, mechanical durability, and device-level integration for wearable and neural interface systems.

Lithography-Based Mesh Electrode

Designed and fabricated micro-patterned mesh electrodes using spin coating, optical lithography (MLA, MABA6), thin film processing, and pattern transfer. Improved feature fidelity and electrical continuity through process optimization and structural validation.

Stick-and-Play Bioadhesive Hairlike EEG Electrode

Developed DIW-fabricated hairlike electrode architecture integrating conductive polymer hydrogel and bioadhesive interface. Validated chronic scalp adhesion and electrophysiological signal acquisition without gel or skin preparation.

Conductive Adhesive Systems

Engineered PU–PAA graft interpenetrating network integrated with PEDOT:PSS for conformal skin-contact electronics. Performed chemical, mechanical, and electrical characterization to evaluate interfacial reliability and device stability.

Nanofiber-Based Drug Delivery Systems

Developed nanofiber membranes using centrifugal spinning (Forcespinning). Investigated processing–structure–property relationships of PLGA, PVA, pullulan/chitosan, and composite systems. Performed thermal, structural, and biological characterization for biomedical applications.
